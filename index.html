About Me

I’m originally from Mexico, I started working in as a Java Developer in the year 2000, In 2002 I moved to Toronto, Canada, where I continued working as a java developer but eventually made my transition to .NET.

In 2005 I had the opportunity to get involved in the architecting of a major enterprise sales system, I move temporarily to Switzerland and worked at the Philip Morris International headquarters. The technical challenges made the company a great place to work, Working for PMI was the best professional experience in my whole career.

Eventually, I moved to the United States and made Los Angeles my new home. I worked at Fox Interactive Media and MySpace for almost 4 years, that was fun and challenging.

After MySpace I worked for Verizon Digital Media Services – (Edgecast), where I was the Lead Developer for the Portals-Core vertical, I had the opportunity to work on their billing system, reporting engine and API platform, I was also given the opportunity to lead successfully a very high profile project with a corporation in Redmond, this yielded a corporate award to my team.

In June 2014 I decided to leave Verizon to make a pause in my career, I decided to spent time learning about other engineering areas and photography.

I gave myself a new challenge and decided to switch back to open source platforms, I worked at Spark Networks briefly and then at JibJab Bros Studios, which I left in July 2016.
